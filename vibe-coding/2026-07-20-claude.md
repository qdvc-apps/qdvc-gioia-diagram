## 1. User request

```csv

```

This is a hierarchy of M:1 .. M:1. In other words, multiple instances of `first_order_concept`​ belong to a single `second_order_theme`​, and multiple instances of `second_order_theme​` belong to a single `aggregate_dimension`​.

The second-order theme enumerates the first-order concepts associated with it. For instance the second-order theme "Interpretation based on inadequate information" is the first of that level, so its associated first-order concepts are enumerated 1A. Absence of verbal backchannels, 1B. Interpreting with abnormal eye contact (eccentric gaze), etc. (cycling 1A, 1B ... 1Z, assuming no more than 26 first-order themes for a given second-order concept).

The diagram itself consists of three vertical panels with a light grey background. At the top of each panel its heading: First-order concepts, Second-order themes, Aggregate dimensions.

From right to left:
- Aggregate dimensions are drawn as ovals with a black background and white text in bold (panel 3). Its associated second-order themes are separated rectangles with white backgrounds and black text in bold.
- A set of second-order theme rectangles (panel 2) is connected to its associated aggregate dimension oval (panel 3) with solid black lines. The set of second-order theme rectangles should be vertically centre-aligned with its associated aggregate dimension oval. If the number of second-order themes associated with an aggregate dimension is odd, then the middle item should be vertically centre-aligned with the aggregate dimension. If the number of second-order themes associated with an aggregate dimension is even, then the middle 2 items should be vertically centre-aligned with the aggregate dimension.
- Each second-order theme (panel 2) is vertically aligned with its set of first-order concepts (panel 1), which is also a rectangle with a white background and black text, but this text is of regular weight rather than bold. The second-order theme (panel 2) rectangle and its set of first-order concepts (panel 1) rectangle must be of equal height; whichever one would have been shorter should be expanded to match.
- There is to be a large grey arrowhead pointing from the first-order concepts box to the second-order concepts box.  
- Within the rectangle containing the set of first-order concepts (panel 1), each of the first-order concepts is listed by enumeration and name.
- All boxes and ovals described above have text that is vertically and horizontally centre-aligned within its shape. All boxes and ovals should also have a drop shadow.

A sample diagram is available in Figure 1 of Wang and Prester (2025) - Figure 1.

References:

Gioia, D. A., Corley, K. G., & Hamilton, A. L. (2013). Seeking Qualitative Rigor in Inductive Research. Organizational Research Methods, 16(1), 15–31. https://doi.org/10.1177/1094428112452151
